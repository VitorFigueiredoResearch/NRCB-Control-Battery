## Figure 1

**Control workflow for radial-structure diagnostics.** This workflow separates the `N=134` partial-rank branch from the `N=76` spectral-control branch. Shuffle/permutation controls and exhaustive circular-shift controls are stochastic/control-distribution branches. Random-phase spectral controls carry a phase-blind caveat. Smooth profiles are fixed references, not stochastic controls. The QC/language-lock step prevents physical or manuscript-level claims before audit.

## Figure 2

**Branch-separated control-distribution summaries.** Panel A shows the `N=134` partial-rank branch: the observed absolute partial-rank statistic and per-galaxy medians from the `M=1000` shuffle and exact exhaustive circular-shift control distributions. Exact circular-shift randomization distributions supersede the earlier single phase-shifted control for production-facing figures. Panel B separately shows the `N=76` spectral-control branch: observed spectral concentration, per-galaxy random-phase control medians, and smooth-profile fixed references. Panels summarize each per-galaxy control distribution by its median; empirical percentiles and p-values are retained in the production summary tables, and the full raw control realizations are not plotted. Fixed smooth references are not stochastic controls.

## Figure 3

**Phase-blind spectral-concentration caveat and fixed references (`N=76`).** Panel A compares observed spectral concentration with per-galaxy medians from amplitude-preserving random-phase controls. The identity-line behavior demonstrates a phase-blind property of the spectral-concentration statistic under amplitude-preserving random-phase controls. The random-phase equality is by construction and is not physical evidence. Random phase, rather than shuffle, is the relevant spectral caveat control because it preserves spectral amplitudes while changing phase. Panel B shows descriptive across-galaxy medians; smooth profiles are fixed references, not stochastic controls.
